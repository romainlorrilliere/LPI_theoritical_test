One population abundance

Exponential dynamic

We generate the abundance variation of one population following an exponential function. Thus the population abundance at t time N_t is only according to : N(t+1) = exp(r) * N(t) N0

For example : r = 0.05 and N0 = 100

or another example : r = -0.05 and N0 = 100

## Temporal variability

Then we add temporal variability in the r parameter.

For example : r in N(mean=0.05,sd = 0.025) and N0 = 100

or another example : r in N(mean=-0.05,sd = 0.025) and N0 = 100

Temporal stochasticity

One could also add a demographic stochasticity process by picking abundance from a Poisson distribution.

with the same paramters: r in N(mean=-0.05,sd = 0.025) and N0 = 100

Several populations

Finally, we can generate the dynamics of as many populations as necessary

for example for 5 populations:

N0 in N(mean=100,sd = 10) and r in N(mean=N(mean = -0.05,sd = 0.02),sd = 0.025)
